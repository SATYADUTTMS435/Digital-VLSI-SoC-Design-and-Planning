# Day 5 – Power Distribution Network (PDN) Generation and Routing

## Introduction

After floorplanning and Clock Tree Synthesis (CTS), the next stage in the OpenLane flow is the implementation of the **Power Distribution Network (PDN)** followed by **global and detailed routing**. The PDN ensures that every standard cell receives stable power (VDD) and ground (VSS), while routing establishes all signal interconnections according to the synthesized netlist. These stages complete the physical connectivity of the design before final verification.

---

# Theory

## Power Distribution Network (PDN)

A **Power Distribution Network (PDN)** is a structured network of power and ground rails that distributes VDD and VSS across the entire chip. It consists of power rings, straps, and standard-cell rails that minimize voltage drop (IR drop) and ensure reliable power delivery during circuit operation.

---

## Signal Routing

Routing is the process of connecting all logical nets using the available metal layers while satisfying Design Rule Check (DRC) constraints. OpenLane performs routing in two stages:

- **Global Routing** – Estimates routing paths and allocates routing resources.
- **Detailed Routing** – Generates the final legal wire geometry while satisfying all technology rules.

---

## TritonRoute

OpenLane uses **TritonRoute** for detailed routing. It creates DRC-clean routing by connecting all nets using the available routing layers while minimizing congestion and maintaining design-rule compliance.

---

# Loading the Routed Design in Magic

The routed DEF generated by OpenLane can be viewed in Magic using the merged technology LEF and DEF files.

```tcl
magic -T <PDK>/libs.tech/magic/sky130A.tech \
lef read ../../tmp/merged.nom.lef \
def read picorv32a.def &
```

This command loads the routed design with all technology layers and standard-cell definitions into Magic for physical inspection.

---

# Power Distribution Network Generation

OpenLane automatically generates the Power Distribution Network after Clock Tree Synthesis.

```tcl
gen_pdn
```

During this stage OpenLane creates:

- Core power rings
- Horizontal and vertical power straps
- Standard-cell power rails
- Connections between macros and power network

A well-designed PDN minimizes voltage drop and provides uniform power delivery throughout the chip.

---

# Routing

Once the PDN is generated, signal routing is performed.

```tcl
run_routing
```

Routing consists of two major phases:

1. **Global Routing**
   - Determines approximate routing paths.
   - Estimates routing congestion.
   - Allocates routing resources.

2. **Detailed Routing**
   - Creates the final wire geometry.
   - Resolves routing conflicts.
   - Produces a DRC-clean routed layout.

---

---

## Zooming into the Routed Design

By zooming into different regions of the layout, individual routing tracks, vias, and standard cells become visible. The routing network connects all logic cells while maintaining spacing and enclosure rules defined by the SKY130 technology.

---

## Standard Cells and Routing

At higher magnification the following can be observed:

- Standard cells
- Metal routing layers
- Signal interconnections
- Power rails
- Clock buffers
- Decap cells
- Filler cells

These cells together complete the physical implementation of the synthesized design.

---

## Decap Cells

Decoupling capacitor (Decap) cells are inserted throughout the design to reduce supply voltage fluctuations caused by switching activity. They improve power integrity by acting as local charge reservoirs.

---

## Filler Cells

Filler cells occupy unused placement gaps between standard cells. They maintain N-well and substrate continuity, satisfy manufacturing requirements, and ensure proper power rail continuity across the design.

---

## Clock Buffers

Clock buffers inserted during CTS remain visible after routing. They distribute the clock signal with controlled skew while maintaining timing across all sequential elements.
